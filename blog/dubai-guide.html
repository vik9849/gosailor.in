Dubai Travel Guide 2026: Everything You Need to Know

📅 April 2026⏱ 8 min read✍ GoSailor Travel Team

Dubai is one of the most visited cities in the world — and for Indian travelers, it is one of the easiest international destinations to reach. With direct flights from almost every major Indian city, a visa on arrival for Indian passport holders, and a city built for tourism, Dubai delivers a world-class experience at surprisingly accessible prices if you plan right.

💡 Visa tip: Indian passport holders can get a UAE visa on arrival for 14 days (free) or apply for a 30-day tourist visa online for approximately ₹3,500–₹5,000.

Best Things to Do in Dubai

  • 🏙 Burj Khalifa — Visit the world's tallest building. Book tickets in advance from Klook to skip the queue and save money
  • 🛍 Dubai Mall — World's largest mall, completely free to visit
  • 🌊 Dubai Fountain — Spectacular free show every evening
  • 🏜 Desert Safari — Dune bashing, camel riding, dinner under the stars
  • 🚡 Dubai Frame — Stunning views of old and new Dubai
  • 🛥 Dubai Marina — Beautiful waterfront, yacht tours available

Where to Stay in Dubai

Dubai has accommodation options for every budget, from backpacker hostels to ultra-luxury hotels. The best areas to stay are Deira (budget), Bur Dubai (mid-range) and Downtown Dubai (splurge).

Dubai Travel Tips for Indian Travelers

  • Best time to visit: November to March (cooler weather)
  • Avoid June to August — extremely hot, up to 45°C
  • Hindi and Urdu are widely spoken — very easy for Indians
  • Indian food is everywhere — plenty of vegetarian options
  • Carry dirhams (AED) — exchange rate is approximately ₹23 per AED
  • Use the Dubai Metro — cheap and air-conditioned
