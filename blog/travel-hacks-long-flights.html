25 Travel Hacks for Long Flights That Actually Work in 2026

📅 May 2026⏱ 12 min read✍ GoSailor Travel Team🔥 150% trending

The difference between arriving exhausted and arriving refreshed is almost entirely about preparation. These 25 hacks are tested by frequent flyers on routes of 10+ hours — and they actually work.

✈️ First hack: Use SeatGuru.com to pick the best seat on your exact aircraft before checking in. A good seat makes everything else easier.

Before You Board

1

Book a window seat

Control the shade, lean against the wall, avoid being woken up. Non-negotiable for sleeping on flights over 8 hours.

2

Check in exactly 24 hours before

Airlines release premium seats (extra legroom, exit rows) at check-in. Log in exactly at the 24-hour mark to grab the best free upgrades.

3

Download everything offline

Download Netflix, Spotify and podcasts before you fly. In-flight WiFi is expensive and unreliable. 20 hours of content = full control.

4

Pack your own snacks

Airline food timing is unpredictable. Nuts, protein bars and dark chocolate keep you satisfied without the bloating.

5

Bring an empty water bottle

Fill it at any water fountain after security. Saves £3–5 per bottle at airport shops.

Sleeping on the Plane

6

Set watch to destination time immediately

The moment you board, set your phone to your destination's time zone and start mentally living in it. Dramatically reduces jet lag.

7

Take melatonin at destination bedtime

0.5mg taken at your destination's bedtime helps reset your circadian rhythm. Much gentler than sleeping pills.

8

Eye mask + noise cancelling headphones

These two items transform a long flight. Active noise cancellation removes the constant engine roar that is exhausting over 10+ hours.

9

Avoid alcohol completely

Cabin pressure makes alcohol's effects more intense and dramatically worsens jet lag. One drink on a plane equals two on the ground.

10

Dress in removable layers

Plane cabins fluctuate between hot and freezing. Compression socks underneath help prevent DVT on flights over 6 hours.

Comfort and Health

11

Drink 250ml of water per hour

Airplane cabins have humidity of just 10–20% — drier than most deserts. Dehydration causes headaches, fatigue and worse jet lag.

12

Walk the aisle every 2 hours

DVT is a real risk on long flights. Walk to the back, do 20 calf raises, roll your ankles. Compression socks further reduce risk.

13

Take shoes off but keep socks on

Your feet swell on long flights. Remove shoes immediately. But always keep socks on — aircraft floors are not clean.

14

Use moisturiser and lip balm

The dry cabin air dehydrates your skin rapidly. Apply moisturiser after takeoff. Your skin will thank you on arrival.

15

Eat light before and during the flight

Heavy meals make jet lag worse. Eat light before boarding and choose lighter options from the meal service.

Getting Upgrades

16

Dress smartly and ask politely at check-in

Airlines occasionally upgrade passengers who look like they belong in business class. Be polite, ask if upgrades are available, smile.

17

Travel solo

Airlines are more likely to upgrade a single passenger than two people together. One empty business seat is easier to fill.

18

Join frequent flyer programs

Even flying once a year — being a member shows in their system. Status members get priority for upgrades.

Saving Money on Long-Haul Flights

19

Search in incognito mode always

Booking sites track searches and raise prices when you return. Private browsing removes this and often shows lower fares.

20

Book connecting flights separately

Sometimes two separate one-way tickets via a hub is significantly cheaper than a through ticket. Compare both on GoSailor.

21

Fly Tuesday or Wednesday

Mid-week flights are consistently 15–25% cheaper than Friday, Sunday and Monday on the same routes.

22

Check the airline directly after comparing

Use GoSailor to find the cheapest fare, then check the airline's website — sometimes they offer extras direct at the same price.

Arrival Hacks

23

Stay awake until local bedtime on arrival

The hardest but most effective jet lag cure. Push through until 10pm local time. You'll wake up fully adjusted.

24

Book airport transfer in advance

After 15 hours of flying, the last thing you want is to figure out transport. Klook offers pre-booked transfers at most major airports.

25

Screenshot insurance and accommodation details

If your phone dies or you lose data on arrival — you still have everything you need saved offline.
