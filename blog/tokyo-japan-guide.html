Tokyo Travel Guide 2026: The Ultimate Japan Experience

📅 April 2026⏱ 7 min read✍ GoSailor Travel Team

Tokyo is the #2 most searched travel destination globally in 2026 — and once you visit, you instantly understand why. This hyper-modern megalopolis blends ancient temples with neon-lit streets, world-class sushi with Michelin-starred ramen, and bullet trains with centuries-old tea ceremonies. There is simply nowhere else on earth like Tokyo.

💡 Visa info: Most nationalities need a Japan tourist visa. Apply at your nearest Japanese embassy — usually takes 5–7 working days. Fee approximately ₹1,500. US, UK, EU citizens get visa-free entry for 90 days.

Top Things to Do in Tokyo

  • ⛩ Senso-ji Temple Asakusa — Tokyo's oldest temple, free entry, stunning at dawn
  • 🗼 Tokyo Skytree — World's tallest tower, observation deck from ¥2,100
  • 🎮 Akihabara — Electric Town, anime, gaming paradise, free to explore
  • 🌸 Shinjuku Gyoen — Beautiful garden, cherry blossoms in April, entry ¥500
  • 🍣 Tsukiji Outer Market — World's best sushi breakfast, from ¥500
  • 🎌 Shibuya Crossing — World's busiest pedestrian crossing, completely free
  • 🏯 Meiji Shrine — Peaceful forest shrine in the heart of Tokyo, free entry

Tokyo Budget Guide

  • Budget guesthouse/hostel: from ¥2,500/night (approx $17)
  • Ramen bowl: ¥800–¥1,200 (approx $6–8)
  • Tokyo Metro day pass: ¥600 (approx $4)
  • Convenience store meal (7-Eleven): ¥400–¥700

Best Time to Visit Tokyo

  • March–April: Cherry blossom season — most magical but very busy
  • October–November: Autumn foliage, perfect weather, fewer crowds
  • June–August: Hot and humid, but summer festivals are incredible
  • December–February: Cold but quiet, best for budget travelers
