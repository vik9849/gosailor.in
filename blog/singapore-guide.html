Is Singapore Safe for Solo Travelers? The Complete 2026 Honest Guide

📅 April 2026⏱ 10 min read✍ GoSailor Travel Team🌍 Updated for 2026

Short answer: Singapore is one of the safest cities in the world for solo travelers — including solo female travelers. It consistently ranks in the global top 3 for safety. But "safe" doesn't mean "no surprises." This guide covers everything a solo traveler actually needs to know before booking their flight.

✅ The verdict: Singapore scores 84/100 on the Global Peace Index 2026. Crime rates are among the world's lowest. Solo travelers — male or female — report feeling completely comfortable walking alone at any hour. It is arguably the best city in Asia to visit solo for the first time.

Why Singapore is Perfect for First-Time Solo Travelers

Singapore removes almost every anxiety a solo traveler has. The MRT (subway) covers the entire city and is impossible to get lost on. English is an official language — every sign, menu and official is English-speaking. The food scene at hawker centres means you're never eating alone awkwardly at a restaurant — you grab a tray and sit wherever you like.

The city is also extremely walkable between major attractions. You can walk from Marina Bay Sands to the Merlion Park to the Gardens by the Bay all in one afternoon without needing to think about transport.

Is Singapore Safe for Solo Female Travelers?

Yes — overwhelmingly. Singapore is consistently ranked as one of the top 5 cities in the world for solo female travel. Women report feeling completely comfortable walking alone at midnight in virtually all areas of the city. The MRT runs until 1am on weekdays and 2am on weekends. Taxis and Grab (Singapore's Uber) are reliable, metered and safe.

That said, the usual sensible precautions apply — don't leave drinks unattended at bars, trust your instincts in any situation, and keep digital copies of your passport and travel insurance.

⚠️ Things that CAN go wrong in Singapore (not safety-related, but worth knowing): Singapore has strict laws. Chewing gum is banned. Jaywalking is a fine. Drug offences carry the death penalty. Vandalism laws are seriously enforced. None of this affects normal tourists — just be aware that Singapore is a rules-following city.

Best Areas to Stay Alone in Singapore

1. Chinatown (Best for Budget Solo Travelers)

Excellent MRT access, surrounded by hawker centres, vibrant atmosphere. Hostels from $25/night. Very safe, always busy.

2. Bugis / Arab Street (Best for Culture)

Colourful, multicultural, great street food. Central location, easy access to everything. Hotels from $70/night.

3. Marina Bay / Clarke Quay (Best Splurge)

Right next to the major attractions. Clarke Quay has amazing nightlife for solo socialising. Hotels from $120/night.

Singapore Visa for Solo Travelers (2026)

Most nationalities need a Singapore tourist visa before arrival. Apply online through the Singapore government's ICA portal at least 2 weeks before your trip. Processing time is 3–5 working days. Fee is SGD 30 (approximately $22 / £18).

Citizens of US, UK, Australia, Canada, EU, Japan, South Korea and most Western countries get visa-free entry for 30–90 days. Check the full list at ICA.gov.sg.

Solo Traveler Tips for Singapore

  • Get an EZ-Link card at the airport — loads onto your phone via the SimplyGo app
  • Eat every meal at hawker centres — same chefs as many restaurants, fraction of the price
  • Download the Grab app before you arrive — cheaper and safer than street taxis
  • Book Gardens by the Bay Cloud Forest in advance — sells out on weekends
  • Best solo travel months: February–April and September–October (lower humidity, fewer crowds)
  • Singapore Changi Airport has free cinema, swimming pool and gardens — arrive early!

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Singapore safe to walk alone at night? +
Yes — Singapore is one of the safest cities in the world at night. Most areas are well-lit and busy until late. The MRT runs until 2am on weekends. Virtually all solo travelers report feeling completely safe walking alone at any hour.
How many days is enough for Singapore solo? +
3–4 days is the sweet spot for a solo trip. You can cover all major free attractions in 2 days, with another day for day trips to Sentosa or the Southern Ridges. 5 days allows for a more relaxed pace.
Is Singapore expensive for solo travel? +
It depends on your choices. Budget solo travelers who eat at hawker centres and stay in hostels can get by on $45–60/day. Mid-range travelers should budget $120–180/day. The biggest cost is accommodation.
What is the best month to visit Singapore solo? +
February–April for the driest weather and comfortable temperatures. Avoid June–August which is peak humidity. December has great festive atmosphere but higher hotel prices.
